How to read departure and arrival windows

The main tool labels a train as scheduled today, en route, or tomorrow based on Pakistan Standard Time. That helps you see whether a published departure is still ahead of you or whether the train should already be inside its scheduled operating window.

  • Use departure time first when you are planning same-day travel.
  • Watch the next-day arrival flag on overnight intercity routes.
  • Compare duration separately from clock time when two trains leave at similar hours.

Useful route patterns

Karachi to Lahore Evening departures are usually easier for overnight travel and morning station arrival planning.
Lahore to Rawalpindi Daytime intercity runs are easier to compare by corridor and departure slot.
Quetta corridor Longer western routes benefit from checking duration and endpoint timing together.
